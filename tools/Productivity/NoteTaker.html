Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our Note Taker tool

The Note Taker tool allows you to create, edit, and organize notes with ease. You can categorize notes, format text using bold, italic, or underline, insert lists or dates, and search through your notes by title, content, or category. Notes auto-save as you type, and you can export or import them as JSON files.

Yes, your notes are automatically saved as you type, with a 1-second delay to ensure smooth performance. You can also manually save using Ctrl+S (or Cmd+S on Mac). A backup auto-save occurs every 30 seconds if there are unsaved changes.

No, your privacy is our priority. All notes are stored in your browser's memory and are not sent to our servers, stored, or shared. Notes persist only during your session unless you export them manually. Closing or refreshing the browser clears your notes unless exported.

Yes, you can export all your notes as a JSON file by clicking the "Export Notes" button. To import notes, use the import feature to upload a previously exported JSON file, choosing to merge with existing notes or replace them.

There’s no strict limit to the number of notes you can create. However, performance may vary depending on your device’s memory for very large numbers of notes (e.g., thousands). For most users, the tool handles notes efficiently without issues.

Yes, once the Note Taker page is loaded, all functionality (creating, editing, and managing notes) works offline since processing happens in your browser. You’ll need an internet connection to initially load the page or access updates.

Absolutely! The Note Taker is fully responsive and works seamlessly on smartphones and tablets. The interface adapts to smaller screens, allowing you to create, edit, and organize notes on the go.

Tooli’s Note Taker is free, browser-based, and requires no installation. It offers advanced features like categorization, search, text formatting, and export/import functionality, all while prioritizing your privacy with local storage. It’s perfect for quick note-taking or managing detailed projects.

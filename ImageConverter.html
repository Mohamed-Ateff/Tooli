Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our Image Converter tool

Our converter supports the most common image formats including JPEG, PNG, WebP, GIF, and BMP as input. You can convert to JPEG, PNG, or WebP formats. JPEG and WebP support quality adjustment, while PNG maintains lossless compression.

JPEG is best for photos with many colors and smaller file sizes. PNG supports transparency and is ideal for graphics with few colors. WebP offers superior compression and quality, combining the best of both JPEG and PNG, but has limited support in older browsers.

No, your privacy is completely protected. All image processing happens locally in your browser. Your images are never uploaded to our servers, stored, or shared with anyone. The conversion is performed entirely on your device.

For web use, 80-90% quality offers a good balance of file size and image quality. For printing or archival purposes, use 95-100%. For thumbnails or previews, 60-70% is usually sufficient. Lower quality means smaller file sizes but reduced image quality.

Yes! You can set a maximum width for your converted image. The tool will automatically maintain the aspect ratio while resizing. Leave the max width field empty or set it to a large value to keep the original dimensions.

The maximum file size is 10MB. This limit ensures optimal performance while accommodating most image files. If your image is larger, consider resizing it first or using image compression software before conversion.

WebP provides superior compression compared to JPEG and PNG, often reducing file sizes by 25-35% while maintaining the same quality. It supports both lossy and lossless compression, transparency, and animation. It's perfect for web use and modern applications.

Currently, our tool processes one image at a time to ensure optimal quality and performance. However, you can quickly convert multiple images by repeating the process. Each conversion is fast and maintains your previous settings.
